Introduction Maths

Mathematics is the universal language of logic, structure, and problem-solving. It is the study of numbers, shapes, and patterns, enabling us to model the physical world and establish undeniable truths through precise reasoning and formal proof.

Workshop calculation math comprises the applied mathematics and scientific principles used in trades and engineering to solve practical, on-the-job problems. It bridges theoretical concepts like algebra, geometry, and physics with everyday fabrication, machining, and technical tasks

Fundamental Arithmetic & Algebra

Fractions and Decimals: Converting fractions to decimals for precise measurements.Square Roots: Essential for calculating dimensions, especially using the Pythagorean theorem.Algebraic Formulas: Transposing and solving equations to determine unknown values (e.g., finding the missing side of a triangle or required material sizes)

2. Geometry & Mensuration

Area and Perimeter: Calculating the surface area of sheets or the perimeter for cutting stock (e.g., squares, rectangles, and circles).Circumference: Finding the required length of a workpiece to form a cylinder or circular ring.Volume: Estimating material weights, casting volumes, or fluid capacities of vessels.

  • Newton's Second Law:\(F = m \times a\)(Where \(F\) = Force, \(m\) = mass, \(a\) = acceleration)

  • Work Done:\(W = F \times d \times \cos(\theta)\)(Where \(W\) = Work, \(d\) = distance, \(\theta \) = angle of force)

  • Power:\(P = \frac{W}{t}\)(Where \(P\) = Power, \(t\) = time)

  • Kinetic Energy:\(KE = \frac{1}{2} \times m \times v^2\)(Where \(v\) = velocity)

  • Potential Energy:\(PE = m \times g \times h\)(Where \(g\) = gravity, \(h\) = height)
