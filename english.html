Introduction of English Typing

English typing is the process of inputting text into a computer or typewriter using the standard QWERTY keyboard. Mastering it involves touch-typing—using all ten fingers based on muscle memory without looking down at the keys—which drastically increases your typing speed and efficiency

The Keyboard Layout

Understanding how the keyboard is organized is the first step to mastering English typing. It is primarily divided into three functional zone
The Home Row: The middle letter row (A, S, D, F, J, K, L, ;) where your fingers should rest by default.
The Upper & Lower Rows: Contain additional letters, numbers, and punctuation.
The Special Keys: Modifiers like Shift (for capital letters), Spacebar, Enter, and Backspace

Proper Finger Placement

Touch-typing relies heavily on putting specific fingers in charge of specific keys. This establishes muscle memory and prevents wasted hand movements.
Left Hand Fingers: Place your pinky on 'A', ring finger on 'S', middle finger on 'D', and index finger on 'F'. Your index finger is also responsible for 'G' and 'T
Right Hand Fingers: Place your index finger on 'J', middle finger on 'K', ring finger on 'L', and pinky on ';'. Your index finger also controls 'H' and 'y'
Thumbs: Both thumbs rest on the Spacebar and alternate pressing it depending on which hand just typed the word.
